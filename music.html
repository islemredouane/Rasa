About the Department

The Music Department of our club is a space for passionate artists and music lovers. We explore different genres, instruments, and rhythms that bring people together through sound. Whether you play, sing, or simply enjoy listening, our community celebrates the universal language of music.



On the page below, you’ll find a collection of the musical instruments available in our club. Each one is presented with its name, description, and image — instruments that our members can see, learn about, and use during rehearsals and club activities.

Our Musical Instruments

Guitar

The guitar is a stringed musical instrument played by strumming or plucking the strings with fingers or a pick. It is one of the most versatile instruments, used in various genres such as classical, rock, jazz, and pop. Its expressive sound makes it ideal for both rhythm and melody.

Piano

The piano is a keyboard instrument that produces sound by striking strings with hammers when keys are pressed. Known for its wide range and dynamic expression, it serves as both a solo and accompanying instrument in nearly every style of music.

explore a tutorial

Violin

The violin is a bowed string instrument with four strings, known for its bright and emotional tone. It is used in classical orchestras, folk music, and many modern genres. The violin’s expressive range allows musicians to convey deep emotions through subtle variations in pitch and dynamics.

explore a tutorial

Darbuka

The darbuka is a traditional percussion instrument shaped like a goblet, played with bare hands or fingertips. It produces sharp, resonant tones that form the rhythmic heartbeat of Middle Eastern, North African, and Mediterranean music. Light and expressive, the darbuka brings energy and emotion to any performance, blending rhythm with cultural heritage.

explore a tutorial

Qarqabou

The qarqabou, also known as qraqeb, are large metal castanets used in traditional Gnawa music from Morocco and North Africa. They produce a sharp, metallic rhythm that accompanies chants and melodies, creating a powerful and hypnotic groove. The qarqabou represent both rhythm and spirituality in the region’s musical heritage.

explore a tutorial

Bendir

The bendir is a traditional frame drum widely used in North African and Middle Eastern music. It features a wooden frame with a stretched animal skin or synthetic membrane, often including gut strings across the back to create a distinctive buzzing sound.
The bendir provides rhythmic depth and energy in both spiritual and folk performances.

explore a tutorial

Zurna

The zurna is a traditional woodwind instrument, similar to an oboe, commonly played across North Africa, the Middle East, and Central Asia. It produces a strong, high-pitched, and vibrant sound, often used in celebrations, weddings, and festivals. The zurna’s expressive tone makes it a centerpiece in outdoor musical performances and cultural ceremonies.
